# Research Taxonomy

## AI-Augmented Scientific Discovery
This sub-area focuses on developing intelligent, interactive, and adaptive AI systems that actively assist domain experts in hypothesis generation, data-driven exploration, and iterative scientific inquiry—bridging machine learning with empirical research workflows across disciplines like finance, engineering, and vision science. (n=4)

## Quantum-Enhanced Control
This sub-area focuses on leveraging quantum phenomena—such as dissipation, many-body correlations, and photonic hardware—to design and optimize control strategies for quantum systems, often integrating physics-informed learning and hardware-aware computation. (n=15)

## Computational Physics Integration
This sub-area bridges advanced computational methods—including physics-informed modeling, quantum simulation, hardware-aware inference optimization, and precision micro-fabrication—with physical system understanding and real-world implementation constraints. (n=6)

## Interdisciplinary AI-Human Collaboration
This sub-area investigates the cognitive, affective, and performance dynamics of human-AI collaboration—particularly in programming and problem-solving contexts—using controlled empirical methods to assess learning, workload, emotion, and effectiveness compared to traditional human-human collaboration. (n=10)

## Physics-Informed Digital Twins
This sub-area develops high-fidelity, dynamically responsive digital representations of physical systems—spanning quantum hardware, biological entities, and materials—by tightly integrating first-principles physics, multimodal sensing, and learned spatiotemporal models. (n=9)

## Stochastic Geometric Analysis
This sub-area studies probabilistic and geometric properties of random processes—such as Brownian motion, active particles, or stochastic fields—focusing on emergent geometric quantities (e.g., convex hulls, perimeters, vortex dynamics) and their analytical characterization using tools from stochastic calculus, optimal transport, and non-linear PDEs. (n=6)
